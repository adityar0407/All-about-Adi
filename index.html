As you navigate through my website, you’ll get a glimpse into my journey. Where I am today, how I got here, and the work I’m excited to keep building on. I’m currently in graduate school at the University of Wisconsin–Madison, where I’m learning and researching within the quantum computing space.

I’ve always been drawn to emerging technology. Before graduate school, I worked in product and emerging tech, where I became interested in how complex ideas move from research and engineering into real-world systems. And while AI seems to be everywhere around us today, I’m working in a field that aims to bring about the next revolution in computing through quantum physics.

My current interests sit at the intersection of quantum algorithms, error correction, hardware-dependent resource estimation, and how we can scale quantum systems into something practically useful. Long term, I hope to help bridge the gap between quantum research, industry, and real-world applications.

Outside of research, I’m someone who loves learning, building, asking questions, and connecting with people who are excited about the future of technology.

Feel free to check out some of my work, and I’m always happy to chat about my ongoing research!

Current focus
  • Otten Group Understanding quantum algorithm design from the NISQ era to the early fault-tolerant regime, where devices are improving but still far from ideal.
  • Hardware-aware thinking Designing superconducting resonators and drawing from neutral-atom lab experience to understand how hardware impacts software in the full stack.
  • Open-source tools Creating quantum software tools that make resource estimates and algorithm tradeoffs easier to explore.

Research
